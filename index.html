LexiMark: Robust Watermarking via
Lexical Substitutions to Enhance Membership
Verification of an LLM’s Textual Training Data

Eyal German1, Sagiv Antebi1, Edan Habler1, Asaf Shabtai1, Yuval Elovici1
1Ben-Gurion University of the Negev
arXiv Code

LexiMark embeds high-entropy synonym substitutions that LLMs memorize — enabling reliable post-training membership verification without harming semantics.

Abstract

Large language models (LLMs) can be trained or fine-tuned on data obtained without the owner’s consent. Verifying whether a specific LLM was trained on particular data instances or an entire dataset is challenging. We present LexiMark, a watermarking technique that replaces the top-K highest-entropy words in each sentence with semantically appropriate synonyms of even higher entropy. These subtle substitutions encourage memorization without degrading text quality, enabling powerful black-box membership-inference attacks post-training.

Across six datasets (BookMIA and five subsets of The Pile) and seven open-source LLMs ranging from 160 M to 8 B parameters, LexiMark boosts AUROC by 2.5 – 25 pp over existing methods, preserves meaning (≥ 0.95 cosine similarity on SBERT embeddings) and remains robust to synonym removal, post-training adaptation and instruction-tuning.

Method

LexiMark operates in two phases:

  • Watermark Embedding. For every sentence we (i) rank words by corpus entropy, (ii) select the top-K, and (iii) substitute each with a synonym that yields even higher entropy while retaining syntactic correctness. Substitutions are blocked on stop-words and named entities.
  • Watermark Detection. After the suspect model is trained, we run any standard black-box MIA (e.g. Min-K++ 20%) on the watermarked tokens only. The resulting likelihood gap yields state-of-the-art AUROC with as few as six records.

The approach is stealthy (no unnatural glyphs), robust (survives post-training and mild edits) and portable (no auxiliary models required).
